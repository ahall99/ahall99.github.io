Audio Projects

For much of my life I have been interested in digital music production. Combining knowledge from this decade-long passion with knowledge gained from taking the Real-time Synthesis and Composition (Mus 109L) series with Dr. Curtis Roads at U.C. Santa Barbara, I have written synthesizers, audio effects, and oscilloscope music. If you don't know what oscilloscope music is, I highly recommend this video.

Check out my oscilloscope composition, implemented from scratch with Max for Live/Gen:

To better understand how it works, I made a Shadertoy visualizing the path and waveforms. (Unfortunately, sound shaders cannot be embedded.)

Music production has been a fun hobby I never dove super far into, but some of my work over the years is on my Soundcloud. At one point I got one of my songs published to a small label!

I am currently working on interesting music visualizers, a composition entirely from code as a function of time (i.e. no wavetables, no state storage), and videos about technical and aesthetic perspectives on stereo width and even harmonics.
